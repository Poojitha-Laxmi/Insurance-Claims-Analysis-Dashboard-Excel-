🔍 Overview

This project focuses on analyzing insurance data to understand claim patterns, risk distribution, and potential fraud indicators. The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of business performance by comparing premium collections against claim payouts.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze the relationship between Premium vs Claims
Identify high-risk customer segments
Understand claim severity distribution
Detect potential fraudulent claims
Highlight geographic concentration of claims


📁 Dataset
The dataset contains transaction-level insurance data including:
Policy details
Claim amounts
Customer demographics
Risk segmentation
Incident severity
Location data


📊 Dashboard Features
🔹 KPI Metrics
Total Customers
Total Claim Amount
Average Claim Amount
Total Premium Amount
Net Profit


🔹 Key Visualizations
Premium vs Claims Trend
Tracks claim payouts vs premium collected over time
Risk Segmentation Analysis
Compares claim amounts across High, Medium, and Low risk customers
Claim Severity Analysis
Distribution of Major, Minor, and Total loss claims
Fraud Detection Analysis
Identifies suspicious claims based on business rules
Geographic Analysis
Top 5 states contributing to claim amounts


⚙️ Tools Used
Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables
Pivot Charts
Slicers & Timeline
Data Cleaning & Transformation

🧠 Key Insights
Claim payouts significantly exceed premium collections, indicating potential data aggregation issues or high-risk exposure.
Low-risk segments contribute a high portion of total claims, suggesting possible misclassification or volume-driven impact.
A small percentage of claims flagged as suspicious contribute disproportionately to total claim value.
Certain states dominate claim amounts, highlighting geographic risk concentration.

🚀 How to Use
Download the Excel file
Open in Microsoft Excel
Use slicers to filter by:
Insurance Type
Social Class
Time Period
Interact with dashboard visuals

📌 Conclusion
This dashboard demonstrates how data can be used to uncover patterns in insurance claims, support decision-making, and identify areas of risk and inefficiency.
